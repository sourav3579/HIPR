Points to Ponder

Don't rely solely on Employer Health Insurance:
  1. Limited Coverage: Employers usually offer lower coverage, typically between 1 to 5 lakhs, with group medical policies.
  2. Portability and Renewal Limits: Employer insurance is valid only if you're with the company. Once you leave, you can't transfer or renew the policy.
  3. Co-payment: Some employer insurance policies require co-payment, meaning you must pay a portion of medical expenses out of your pocket when making a claim.
  4. Cashless Facility: For CGHS (Central Government Health Scheme) for central government employees or state government covers, there's no cashless facility. Everything operates on a reimbursement basis, meaning you'll need to have funds available to pay upfront.
Why buying a combination of plans may be a smarter choice for you:
  1. Comprehensive coverage at a lower overall cost: Combining a base health insurance policy with a top-up policy often results in lower premiums with comprehensive coverage compared to having only the base policy.
  2. Tailored Coverage: Base plus top-up plans allow policyholders to customize their insurance coverage to match their specific needs.
  3. Flexibility in Coverage Limits: This means policyholders can choose coverage levels that fit their budget while still providing adequate protection against risks.
Health insurance looks after your lifestyle in a holistic manner:
  1. Ensuring Access to Healthcare: Health insurance ensures that you have unfettered access to crucial healthcare services. From routine check-ups to specialized treatments.
  2. Financial Burdens: Without health insurance, medical expenses can quickly go out of control. Health insurance shields you from financial strain.
  3. Providing Peace of Mind: Knowing that you're covered by health insurance offers invaluable peace of mind.
Getting health insurance when you're young can save you from paying big premiums later:
  1. Lower Premiums: Health insurance costs less for younger people. Getting insurance early creates a record that can keep your premiums lower over time compared to buying the same coverage at a higher cost as you get older. Some plans adjust premiums based on your age.
  2. Coverage against Pre-Existing Disease: If you wait until you're older to get insurance, you might have pre-existing conditions that could lead to higher premiums or even difficulty getting coverage at all.
  3. No Claim Bonus: When you're young and healthy, with a no-claim, increase your coverage limits.
